Blood Grouping

Lecture 1 · فصائل الدم

Introduction — Antigens, Antibodies & Their Reaction

Antigen: any foreign substance that triggers the immune system. Examples: pathogen antigens, tumors, allergens, blood group antigens (A, B, D).

Antibody (Immunoglobulin / Ig): protein-based structure with a key role in immune response.

  • Each antibody targets a specific antigen (Lock & Key principle)
  • Origin → WBCs (B-Lymphocytes)
  • Mechanisms → Neutralization, Opsonization, Activation, Agglutination
  • Types → Ig MAGED: IgM · IgA · IgG · IgE · IgD

Antigen–Antibody Reaction: chemical interaction between antigen and antibody. In blood → Agglutination (clumping).

In blood group context: Antigen = Agglutinogen (on RBC) · Antibody = Agglutinin (in plasma)

Blood Group Testing — Slide Technique

Steps to determine blood group in the laboratory:

  1. Prepare 3 antibody sera: Anti-A, Anti-B, Anti-D
  2. Place 3 drops of patient blood on a glass slide
  3. Add 1 drop of each anti-serum to each blood drop successively
  4. Mix each with a separate toothpick (avoid cross-contamination) and wait
  5. Observe for agglutination (clumping):

Rh System & Erythroblastosis Fetalis

Lecture 1 (cont.) · نظام Rh

Rh Blood Group System

Based on the D antigen: Rh+ve (85%) = genotype DD or Dd; Rh–ve (15%) = genotype dd.

Key difference from ABO:

  • ABO antibodies (Anti-A, Anti-B) → type IgM — naturally present, large, cannot cross placenta
  • Anti-D → type IgG — only formed after exposure to Rh+ve blood, small, CAN cross placenta
Why Rh matters in pregnancy but ABO doesn't: IgM is too large to cross the placenta. IgG is small enough to pass through → fetal danger.

Importance of Blood Groups

1. Medicolegal & Forensic Importance:

  • Disputed Paternity: 2 alleles inherited (one from each parent). A & B are dominant; O is recessive. Can exclude but not prove paternity.
  • Crime Scene Investigation: Blood evidence can exclude suspects or identify unknown bodies.

2. Blood Transfusion — ensures safety and compatibility (see ABO table).

3. Erythroblastosis Fetalis (Hemolytic Disease of Newborn):

  1. 1st Pregnancy (Sensitization): Rh–ve mother + Rh+ve fetus → at delivery, fetal blood leaks into mother → mother forms Anti-D IgG. First baby usually unaffected.
  2. 2nd Pregnancy (Disease): Pre-formed Anti-D IgG crosses placenta → attacks fetal Rh+ve RBCs → hemolysis → anemia, jaundice, kernicterus (bilirubin deposition in basal ganglia — BBB not developed), or fetal death.
Treatment: Exchange transfusion with Rh–ve Group O blood
Prevention: (1) Avoid Rh+ transfusion to Rh– persons; (2) Check parents' blood before pregnancy; (3) Give Anti-D (Rhogam) to Rh–ve mother after delivery to neutralize fetal D antigens before sensitization occurs.

Can the 1st baby be affected? Yes, if prior sensitization from: (a) previous Rh+ blood transfusion, or (b) fetal-maternal hemorrhage during the first pregnancy.

Physiology of Nerve

Lecture 2 · فسيولوجيا الأعصاب

The Neuron

Neuron = structural and functional unit of the nervous system. Three parts:

  • Soma (cell body) — contains nucleus and organelles; metabolic centre
  • Dendrites — receive impulses from other neurons or receptors
  • Axon (nerve fiber) — carries impulses away from cell body → terminal branches → synaptic knobs (contain neurotransmitter vesicles)

Membrane Potential & Action Potential

Lecture 2 (cont.) · جهد الغشاء وجهد الفعل

Membrane Potential & Excitability

Membrane potential = electrical potential difference between inner and outer surfaces of the cell membrane. Responsible for excitability — ability of all living cells to respond to an adequate stimulus.

Stimulus: any environmental change that excites the cell. Minimal stimulus = threshold / rheobase.

Types of stimuli: chemical · thermal · mechanical · electrical

Why electrical stimulus is preferred in labs: (1) Similar to natural stimuli; (2) Easily controlled; (3) No tissue damage; (4) Accurately measured.

Resting Membrane Potential (RMP)

RMP = –70 to –90 mV (inside negative). Two causes:

  1. Selective Permeability (93%, −66 mV):
    • K⁺ efflux >100× Na⁺ influx. K⁺ intracellular = 35× extracellular → K⁺ diffuses out
    • Na⁺ extracellular = 10× intracellular → wants to enter but low Na⁺ permeability → accumulates outside
    • Large organic anions trapped inside → inside stays negative
  2. Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase Pump (7%, −4 mV): Pumps 3 Na⁺ OUT for every 2 K⁺ IN. Works against concentration gradient → needs ATP. Net loss of 1+ charge per cycle → maintains negativity.
Arabic Translation
RMP = -70 إلى -90 mV (الداخل سالب). سببه: (1) النفاذية الانتقائية (93%): K⁺ يخرج أكثر بكثير من دخول Na⁺ + أيونات عضوية سالبة محبوسة داخل الخلية. (2) مضخة Na⁺/K⁺ (7%): تضخ 3 Na⁺ للخارج مقابل 2 K⁺ للداخل = فقد شحنة موجبة = سلبية الداخل.

Action Potential (AP)

Rapid change in membrane potential after a threshold/adequate stimulus. Obeys All-or-Nothing Law. Recorded by: 2 microelectrodes connected to galvanometer or CRO.

Phases:

  1. Resting phase: RMP = –70 to –90 mV
  2. Depolarization: Voltage-gated Na⁺ channels open → Na⁺ rushes in. At –65 mV (firing level): ALL Na⁺ channels activated → rapid rise to +35 mV (reverse polarity). At +35 mV: ALL Na⁺ channels inactivated.
  3. Repolarization: Na⁺ channels close; voltage-gated K⁺ channels open → K⁺ flows out → potential returns toward –70 mV.
  4. Hyperpolarization (after-potential): K⁺ channels remain open slightly past RMP → membrane becomes more negative than –70 mV.
  5. Restoration: Na⁺/K⁺ pump restores ionic balance.
Key voltages: RMP = –70 to –90 mV → Firing level = –65 mV → Isopotential = 0 mV → Peak = +35 mV

Ionic basis summary:

  • Depolarization → Na⁺ inflow (voltage-gated Na⁺ channels)
  • Repolarization → K⁺ outflow (voltage-gated K⁺ channels)
  • Hyperpolarization → slow closure of voltage-gated K⁺ channels
  • Restoration → Na⁺/K⁺ pump
Arabic Translation
جهد الفعل: تغيّر سريع بعد منبّه عتبي. الأساس الأيوني: إزالة الاستقطاب = دخول Na⁺ (قنوات Na⁺ الكهربائية)؛ إعادة الاستقطاب = خروج K⁺ (قنوات K⁺ الكهربائية)؛ فرط الاستقطاب = بطء إغلاق قنوات K⁺؛ الاستعادة = مضخة Na⁺/K⁺.

Autonomic Nervous System

Lecture 3 · الجهاز العصبي اللاإرادي

Nervous System — Full Classification

The Nervous System divides into:

  • Central NS (CNS) → Brain + Spinal Cord
  • Peripheral NS (PNS) → subdivided into:
    • Somatic NS — voluntary → skeletal muscle
    • Autonomic NS (ANS) — involuntary → smooth/cardiac muscle + glands
      → subdivided into: Sympathetic NS · Parasympathetic NS · Enteric NS

Peripheral nerves classified by:

  • Origin: Cranial (12 pairs) or Spinal (31 pairs)
  • Effector: Somatic → skeletal muscles | Autonomic → smooth/cardiac muscles + glands

الجهاز العصبي: مركزي (الدماغ + الحبل الشوكي) + محيطي. الجهاز المحيطي: جسدي (إرادي → عضلات هيكلية) + لاإرادي (غير إرادي → عضلات ملساء وقلبية وغدد). الجهاز اللاإرادي: ودي + شبه ودي + معوي. الأعصاب المحيطية: 12 زوج قحفي + 31 زوج شوكي.

Autonomic Ganglia

Definition: Collection of neurons outside the CNS.

Function: Distribution centers — 1 preganglionic neuron synapses with 8–9 postganglionic neurons (amplification/divergence).

Types:

  • Paravertebral ganglia — alongside vertebral column (sympathetic chain)
  • Collateral ganglia — between vertebral column and target organ
  • Terminal ganglia — within or near the target organ (parasympathetic)
Arabic Translation

Divisions of the ANS — Overview

⚡ Sympathetic (Thoraco-lumbar: T1–L2)
  • Emergency · Fight or Flight · Exercise
  • Catabolic (energy breakdown)
  • Postganglionic NT: Norepinephrine (NE)
  • Ganglia: paravertebral or collateral
  • Short preganglionic, long postganglionic
☯️ Parasympathetic (Cranio-sacral)
  • Cranial: CN III, VII, IX, X
  • Sacral: S2, S3, S4
  • Rest · Digest · Diuresis · Defecation
  • Anabolic (energy storage)
  • Postganglionic NT: ACh
  • Ganglia: terminal (near organ)
  • Long preganglionic, short postganglionic
Enteric NS: A local nervous system located in the wall of the alimentary (GI) tract. Works independently. Controls GI motility, secretion, and blood flow. Considered the "third division" of the ANS.
